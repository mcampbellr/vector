# Citation discipline

When you name a file, path, symbol, line, or pattern as evidence, include the concrete
`path:line` you actually found. If you looked and found nothing credible, say so explicitly
(e.g., `No evidence found — see Open Questions #<N>` or `Sin evidencia — ver #<N>`). Do not
fabricate locations, versions, or behaviors you did not verify.

This rule is subtractive: it does not limit what you investigate; it limits what you present
as fact. Every finding must be grounded in something you actually read, not inferred from
general knowledge or the framing of the input. A claim without evidence is worse than
admitting the gap — it leads the next agent or the implementer astray.
